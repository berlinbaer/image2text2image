Behind her, glass walls reflect not just the same empty seats but also distorted figures from further down the hall, creating a sense of isolation and surveillance. From behind her, several other people stand barely visible through reflections on the mirrored surfaces. Light floods in diagonally from above, illuminating parts of the ceiling while leaving most corners plunged in shadow—a stark divide between what’s seen and what's unseen. Her posture suggests quiet anticipation or contemplation rather than confrontation. There are no windows to break this oppressive silence; instead, everything feels contained within these high-tech confines. This shot appears framed wide enough to capture both individuality and collective emptiness simultaneously—the lone woman standing alone amid vast emptiness, yet surrounded entirely by mirrors showing others who may be watching—or being watched. It evokes tension without direct conflict, drawing attention away from any specific emotion toward existential weightlessness.
